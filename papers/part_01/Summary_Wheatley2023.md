# Full Citation
Wheatley, T., Thornton, M. A., Stolk, A., & Chang, L. J. (2023). The Emerging Science of Interacting Minds. (Journal/venue as in PDF).

# Topic Tags
Interacting Minds; Synchrony; Mentalizing; Social Prediction; Cross‑Modal Dynamics; Representation

# Core Question / Problem
How should we think about the scientific study of *interacting minds* — that is, cognition that arises during real-time social interaction rather than in isolated individuals? The paper asks which mechanisms support interpersonal coordination, synchrony, and shared states, and what computational and neural principles govern interactive cognition.

# Conceptual or Computational Framework
Wheatley et al. synthesize a multi-level framework emphasizing dynamic coupling between agents. They integrate dynamical-systems ideas (coupled oscillators, synchronization), predictive-processing notions (mutual prediction and error minimization), and representation-level accounts (shared representations, alignment). Mapping to Marr’s levels: (a) Computational — coordinate behavior to achieve mutual goals and communication; (b) Algorithmic — mechanisms like predictive coding loops, phase-locking/synchrony, cross‑modal alignment, and learning of interaction contingencies; (c) Implementational — inter-subject neural coupling measurable with hyperscanning and shared representational patterns in mPFC, STS, sensorimotor systems.

# Methods Overview
This is a review/synthesis combining behavioral experiments (e.g., movement synchrony, interpersonal coordination tasks), computational models (coupled oscillators, predictive models), and neuroscientific methods including hyperscanning (EEG/fMRI), representational similarity analyses, and causal perturbations. Stimuli include naturalistic interactions, music/movement tasks, and structured cooperative/competitive games.

# Key Findings / Synthesized Insights
- **Synchrony as mechanism:** Temporal alignment across actions, physiology, and neural signals promotes affiliation, shared understanding, and smoother prediction.
- **Mutual prediction:** Interactions are supported by reciprocal models where each agent predicts the other and updates based on prediction error; this can produce spontaneous alignment.
- **Cross‑modal codes:** Movement and music share dynamic structure that facilitates emotional and action understanding across modalities (ties to Sievers et al.).
- **Neural coupling:** Hyperscanning studies show that interbrain synchrony correlates with cooperative success, shared meaning, and coordinated action; representational alignment in social brain regions supports coordination.

# Interpretation & Significance
The paper reframes social cognition as fundamentally interactive: cognition emerges not only inside single brains but in the coupling across them. This has theoretical consequences (we must model bidirectional prediction and coupling) and methodological consequences (use hyperscanning, naturalistic stimuli, and interaction-friendly tasks). The synthesis argues for plural methods and for transferring tools from dynamical systems and control theory into social cognitive science.

# Computational‑Social‑Cognitive‑Scientist Hat
- **Lewin:** Would welcome the emphasis on person-in-environment dynamics — interacting minds are Lewinian life‑spaces in motion.
- **Marr:** Would ask for clearer computational goals for interaction (e.g., minimize joint prediction error, maximize action coordination) and algorithmic proposals that map to measurable signals.
- **Brunswik:** Would stress ecological cue validity — how reliably do interaction cues predict partner states? The review addresses this via cross‑modal and synchrony evidence.

# Teaching Hooks
- Live demo: clap-in-time exercise with students to feel synchrony effects on rapport. 
- Analogy: coupled metronomes that gradually synchronize — use short video or in-class demo.

# Pedagogical Lens
- Conceptual difficulty: 3–4/5
- Pre‑requisites: basic dynamics (phase, frequency), predictive coding, basics of RSA/hyperscanning
- Common misconception: interbrain synchrony means two minds merge — emphasize it’s correlation (and sometimes causally relevant) not identity.
- Prompt: "How could you test whether synchrony causes affiliation or affiliation causes synchrony?" (design experiment)

# Concept Graph
- Action timing ↔ Partner prediction because aligned timing reduces mutual prediction error. 
- Cross‑modal dynamics → shared meaning because dynamic structure is a modality-general code. 
- Neural coupling ↔ behavioral synchrony because brain-to-brain alignment accompanies coordinated behavior.
